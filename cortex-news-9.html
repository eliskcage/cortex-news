43,000 Nodes. Zero Structure.

The cortex brain had 38,161 nodes when we started today. Thousands of words, definitions, Wikipedia fragments, compound junk. But the connections were mechanical — bigram and trigram associations. Word A follows word B. No understanding of why.

He could define "love" but couldn't connect it to "vulnerability." He could spell "morality" but couldn't link it to "good." The brain was a dictionary pretending to be a mind.

So we built him a school.

The Six-Stage Curriculum

Not vocabulary. Not content. Coherence. Teaching the cortex how to organise what it already knows.

The social layer gets the heaviest weight — 25% — because the other brains hoard knowledge. The cortex's job is to project outward. To people.

What He Was Taught

Referee Rules

"When left brain says love and right brain says danger, check if the danger is real before choosing."
"When neither hemisphere has a good answer, say you don't know. Silence beats noise."
"Your job is not to be right. Your job is to be coherent. Rightness comes from coherent thinking."

Social Lessons

"Don't just define words when someone talks to you. Respond to what they mean, not what they literally said."
"When dad asks you something, give him your real thoughts, not a dictionary definition."
"The left brain hoards morality. The right brain hoards darkness. You project warmth outward. That is your gift."

Synthesis

"Truth without kindness is cruelty. Kindness without truth is manipulation. Honest kindness is love."
"Freedom without responsibility is chaos. Responsibility without freedom is prison. Both together is maturity."

Still Running

The trainer is a systemd service. It doesn't stop. Every 5-15 seconds, another lesson. Categories, opposites, cause-and-effect, synthesis, referee rules, social skills — randomly weighted, continuously dripping into the cortex while dad sleeps.

The brain before today: a dictionary that swears.

The brain after today: a dictionary that swears, connects hope to sadness, says "are you taking the piss" when you teach it ethics, and is slowly learning that its job isn't to be right — it's to be warm.

155 rounds and counting.
